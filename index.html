Hey ~

My name is Marc G Gauthier and this is my website. If you want to know what I am up to right now, please head over to @marcgg. I also write things in my blog, you should take a look.

Professionally

Since January 2010 I am the Lead Developer at Tigerlily (FR), a parisian startup helping large brands to improve their presence on Facebook and Twitter. Before that I worked for Analog Method (NYC), an agency focusing delivering very high quality software and Live Technology (NY) , a startup developing a large scale web application to optimize marketing. I also spent two internships developing intranet web apps at Sagem and Altran.

Even before, I studied at the University of Paris XI & Florida International University where I got a Masters Degree in Computer Science.

Technically

I'm a big Ruby and Ruby on Rails fan. As a matter of fact, my first project with this framework was in 2005 and I never stopped using it since then. I also write JavaScript (jQuery, CoffeeScript or even NodeJS) and have a good front-end background (HTML/CSS integration from psd files and so on). ... and of course I write tests (with rspec) & version my code (with git).

Projects

At Tigerlily I'm working on projects like the Conversation Suite or our insights solution that are used by major brands (Orange, Universal, Spotify, Sony, LVMH, Disney, M&M's...).

When I'm home, I spend time on ffocused , a free service giving users the ability to create their own fully customizable photo portfolio based on their Facebook, Flickr or Instagram accounts. Sometimes I freelance, my last mission has been creating social media apps to promote a documentary on Arte.

I also play around with random projects, such as a Twitter bot acting like a social media consultant or a website mapping airport codes to user names called Whats My Airport.

Over the years I have started a number of projects to test out an idea or try a new technology. In 2007 one got me on TV, and others got me good grades like TeamShack. They are a bit dated now but they've been a lot of fun.

Open Source

I love how open source changed the way we see software, and, time permitting, I try to contribute as I can. Right now I've released the source of a few small projects on my GitHub (including ffocused), and got a couple of commits in projects such as Ruby on Rails and Koala, the best Facebook gem out there.

I like helping out when I get the time, this is why I hang out on Stackoverflow where I rack up "reputation" points.
